# The Political Lens — YouTube Growth & SEO Strategy

## Channel Vision

Build "The Political Lens" into a trusted daily political analysis channel using AI-powered production with strong human editorial judgment. Target: 1,000 subscribers in 6 months, monetization-ready.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2)

### Content Cadence
- 1 long-form video daily (Mon-Fri), each 3-5 minutes
- 3-5 YouTube Shorts per week (repurposed from long-form highlights)
- 1 Community Tab post per day (polls, breaking news reactions)

### Content Pillars (Rotate Weekly)
1. **Breaking News Analysis** — React to today's biggest political story
2. **Policy Explainers** — "What the new [bill/ruling] actually means for you"
3. **Power Moves** — Who gained/lost political capital this week
4. **Money & Politics** — Follow the money: lobbying, spending, economic impact
5. **Weekend Recap** — Friday summary of the week's biggest stories

### SEO Foundations
- Every title must include the specific person, event, or bill name
- Primary keyword in the first 30 characters of the title
- Tags strategy: first tag = exact target keyword, mix 40% long-tail variations, minimum 10 tags per video
- Description: 150-300 words with primary keyword in opening sentence, timestamps for all sections, 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end
- Always include the current year (2026) in at least 2 tags for recency signals

---

## Phase 2: Growth Engine (Months 3-4)

### Content Clusters
Build topical authority by creating interconnected content series:

- **"What Just Happened"** — Same-day reaction to breaking political news
- **"Explained in 3 Minutes"** — Quick policy explainers optimized for search
- **"The Real Story Behind..."** — Deeper context pieces that link back to breaking coverage

Each long-form video should generate 2-3 related Shorts. Channels using this hybrid approach grow 41% faster.

### Thumbnail & Title A/B Testing
YouTube now supports A/B testing up to 3 thumbnails per video. Use this to test:
- Text-heavy vs. minimal text thumbnails
- Red accent vs. white background emphasis
- Question-based vs. statement-based titles

### Shorts Strategy
- Shorts get distributed regardless of subscriber count (separate recommendation engine)
- Best performing political Shorts: strong opening hook (first 2 seconds), single surprising fact, end with "follow for more"
- Post Shorts at different times than long-form to maximize reach windows
- Never post a Short and long-form at the same time

---

## Phase 3: Authority (Months 5-6)

### Community Building
- Reply to every comment in the first 24 hours (this is the algorithm signal window)
- Pin the most insightful viewer comment (not your own promotion) — this boosts reply rate by ~30%
- Ask thought-provoking questions at the end of each video: "What do you think happens next?" instead of "Like and subscribe"
- Run weekly polls on the Community Tab to make viewers feel like co-creators

### Playlist Strategy
- Create playlists by content pillar (Breaking News, Explainers, etc.)
- Create event-specific playlists when major political events happen (elections, hearings, crises)
- Playlists increase session watch time, which the algorithm rewards heavily

### Cross-Promotion
- Share Shorts on TikTok and Instagram Reels with watermark
- Clip key moments for X/Twitter with link back
- Maintain consistency — same branding, same voice, same upload schedule

## Title & Thumbnail Playbook

### Title Formulas That Work for Political Content
1. **[Person] Just [Action] — Here's What It Means** (e.g., "Biden Just Vetoed the Border Bill — Here's What It Means")
2. **The [Adjective] Truth About [Topic]** (e.g., "The Shocking Truth About the New Tax Plan")
3. **Why [Event] Changes Everything** (e.g., "Why This Supreme Court Ruling Changes Everything")
4. **[Number] Things [Topic] Means for You** (e.g., "3 Things the New Tariffs Mean for Your Wallet")
5. **[Topic] Explained in [Time]** (e.g., "The Border Crisis Explained in 4 Minutes")

### Title Rules
- 50-60 characters maximum (truncated on mobile otherwise)
- Title and thumbnail are a single unit — complement, don't repeat
- Never use ALL CAPS for entire titles (one word max for emphasis)
- Avoid clickbait — content must match expectations or the algorithm penalizes you

### Thumbnail Design (Already Built Into the Renderer)
- Bold 2-3 word text, vertically centered
- Dark background with red accent (brand consistent)
- Category badge for instant topic recognition
- Drop shadow on text for readability at any size
- Design for mobile first (50%+ of YouTube traffic)

## Algorithm Compliance & Risk Management

### AI Disclosure (Critical — July 2025 Policy)
- YouTube requires mandatory disclosure for realistic AI-generated content
- Political content with AI elements requires visible labels on the video player
- Add to every video description: "This video uses AI-generated voiceover and AI-assisted script writing. All topics and editorial angles are selected by the channel creator."
- Never create content that could be mistaken for a real person speaking

### Avoiding "Inauthentic Content" Flag
YouTube's July 2025 policy targets mass-produced, templated content. To stay compliant:
- Maintain genuine human editorial input in topic selection and angle
- Each video should feel distinct, not formulaic
- Vary video structure — don't use the exact same template every single day
- Periodically update the visual style (new overlay designs, different transitions)
- Quality over quantity — skip a day rather than publish low-effort content

### Political Content Risks
- YouTube can rank down channels with divisive political content
- Stay analytical and factual rather than partisan or inflammatory
- Present multiple perspectives when covering controversial topics
- Avoid synthetic content that mimics real politicians speaking
- Never use AI to generate fake quotes attributed to real people

## Key Metrics to Track

### First 30 Days
- Impressions (are titles/thumbnails getting shown?)
- Click-through rate (target: 4-6% initially, 8%+ goal)
- Average view duration (target: 50%+ of video length)
- Subscriber conversion rate per video

### Months 2-6
- Returning viewers percentage (measures loyalty)
- External traffic sources (search, suggested, browse)
- Comment rate and sentiment
- Shorts-to-long-form funnel (do Short viewers watch full videos?)

### Monetization Threshold
- 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours (long-form) OR 10 million Shorts views in 90 days
- At 1 video/day averaging 200 views with 3-min watch time: ~30 hours/month → 4,000 hours in ~4.5 months
- Shorts can accelerate this significantly
